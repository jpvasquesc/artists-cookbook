Steak Tartare

Contribution by Marcel Duchamp. Invented by the Cossacks, in Siberia.

Can be prepared on horseback, at swift gallop, if conditions make this a necessity.

Steps

  1. Chop one half pound (per person) of the very best beef obtainable.
  2. Shape it carefully with artistry into a bird’s nest.
  3. Place on porcelain plate of a solid color — ivory is the best setting — so that no pattern will disturb the distribution of ingredients.
  4. In hollow center of nest, permit two egg yolks to recline.
  5. Like a wreath surrounding the nest of chopped meat, arrange on border of plate in small, separate bouquets:
    • Chopped raw white onion
    • Bright green capers
    • Curled silvers of anchovy
    • Fresh parsley, chopped fine
    • Black olives minutely chopped in company with yellow celery leaves
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Consumption

  • Each guest , with his plate before him, lifts his fork and blends the ingredients with the egg yolks and meat.
  • In center of table: Russian pumpernickel bread, sweet butter, and bottles of vin rosé.
